The Place

Sakai sits at the mouth of the Yamato river, south of Ōsaka. In 1543, the first matchlocks in Japan came ashore here. The smiths who had been forging temple locks turned to gun barrels — then, when peace returned, to kitchen knives. An Ozu first took hammer to iron in 1912. By then the town had been forging blades for nearly four hundred years.

The Lineage

Ozu Gensuke

Founder. Opened the first forge on Tenjin-machi in 1912.

“I came to Sakai with my father’s hammer. The forge was cold for three days before it would take heat.”

1914 – 1994

Second hand. Rebuilt the forge after the war.

  2. 1450°C, folded over and over.

    Hammered out from the chunk, both ends still rounded.

  3. Shaped by hand, no mold.

  4. Quenched in mountain water.

  5. Polished on natural stones, seven grades.

    Surface smoothed, blade geometry resolves.

  6. Handled in magnolia, wrapped in ray skin.
